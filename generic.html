Who We Are

"One person can make a difference, and everyone should try." - John F. Kennedy

Executive Summary:

Cell-Block Creative is a non-profit organization aimed at providing prisoners with entrepreneurial skills and opportunities to showcase their artistic talents, while also generating income and fostering self-sufficiency. The program will offer resources, mentorship, and support to help prisoners turn their creativity into a sellable product.

Our program will support prisoners in developing a entrepreneurial mindset and skills that align with their values and interests through art sales.

The team behind Cell-Block Creative is currently in its development and networking phase and is seeking team members and partners who share our vision for creating a more equitable and supportive society. With the right resources and support, we believe that this program has the potential to make a significant impact on the lives of prisoners and their families, as well as on the larger community.

Problem Analysis:

Lack of support for prisoner reentry: Upon release, prisoners often face numerous challenges such as lack of employment, housing, and support systems. These challenges can contribute to recidivism and hinder their ability to successfully reintegrate into society.

Limited opportunities for personal and professional growth: Prisons often provide limited opportunities for prisoners to develop new skills and interests, or to engage in meaningful work. This lack of opportunity can lead to boredom, depression, and a sense of hopelessness.

Inadequate rehabilitation programs: Many current rehabilitation programs in prisons are limited in scope and do not address the underlying issues that contribute to criminal behavior.

Stigma surrounding ex-offenders: Ex-offenders often face discrimination and stigma in their communities, which can make it difficult for them to secure employment, housing, and other basic necessities.

Market Analysis:

Identify Target Market: Our program is designed for incarcerated individuals who have an interest in developing their entrepreneurial skills and showcasing their artistic talents. Our sales will likely engage those who would like to support a good cause as well as those appreciate the art. There does appear to be a movement among some musicians and other influencers for prison items. For example, Marc Jacobs featured 'Prison Shoes' that sell in excess of 300usd. The problem here is that not only does the money not support prisoner rehabilation, it also glorifies prison. The organization considers this an antithesis to our core values

Competitor Analysis: Currently, there are few programs that focus on providing prisoners with entrepreneurship skills and opportunities to sell their art. This creates a unique opportunity for our program to fill a gap in the market. The market also appears both primed and undersaturated.

Market Trends: The demand for unique, handmade products continues to grow, especially in the online marketplace. This trend presents a promising opportunity for our program to reach a wider audience and generate more sales.

Customer Segments: Our program is designed to support prisoners who are looking to improve their financial situation and find a creative outlet for self-expression. We will target prisoners who have shown a strong interest in developing their entrepreneurial skills and who have a talent for art.

Market Size: The market size is difficult to determine due to limited data on the number of prisoners who are interested in entrepreneurship and art. However, we believe that our program has the potential to reach a large segment of the incarcerated population.

Market Growth: The market for handmade, unique products is growing and we expect this trend to continue in the future. By providing prisoners with the resources and support they need to turn their creativity into a successful business, we believe that our program has the potential to grow rapidly in the coming years. We strive to make art a part of the program rather than the sole emphasis. For this reason, business awareness will be a foundational aspect of the program.

Marketing and Sales Strategy:

Marketing: We will use a multi-channel approach to reach our target market. Our marketing efforts will focus on leveraging social media platforms, such as Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter, to create awareness about our program and generate interest from potential customers. We will also engage in direct mailing to certain individuals who may help promote the products [please refer to footer for more info]. It may be the case that early on the project enlists the help of a marketing management company. As of this writing, there are numerous, well reviewed and accoladed companies that can assist in every price range the company needs as scaling occurs.

Advertising: We will use targeted advertising on social media platforms to reach a wider audience. We will also place advertisements in relevant magazines and online publications to reach potential customers and partners. We also hope to have influencers within our community give a promotional helping hand.

Sales: Our sales strategy will focus on leveraging online marketplaces, such as Etsy and Amazon, to reach a wider audience and generate more sales. We will also work with local businesses and organizations to sell our products in their stores.

Pricing: Our prices will be competitive with similar products in the market. We will also offer discounts and promotions to customers who purchase multiple products or refer friends and family to our program.

Revenue Model: Our revenue model is based on a commission model. We will take a percentage of the sales generated from each product sold. This model will allow us to generate income while also providing prisoners with a fair return for their efforts. We are still in the planning phase of distributions.

Operations:

Supply Chain Management: We will work with suppliers to ensure that we have a steady supply of materials for our prisoners to use in their art projects. Examples of this include shoes, brushes, canvases, paint, overalls etc. Shoes and canvases are examples of more expensive items. With shoes ranging from 5-15 dollars, you can see that a session of five prisoners might cost fifty dollars on an expensive day [if CBC supplies]. We will also work with shipping and logistics companies to ensure that our products reach customers in a timely and cost-effective manner. In order to save money, early iterations of the project may be hosted on a site like BigCommerce to minimize costs on the ramp up. During this time, Stripe and Paypal will be the exclusive payment gateways. As the business scales, a permanent site will be built and hosted on a major cloud provider. The benefits to this are enormous at scale. At scale, more than 5000mth/usd, we would switch to Payment Depot or Stax as the gawteway payment method. If you use an ecommerce server, you are otherwise locked into the former high interest gateways like Stripe. Furthermore, this will allow for integration of other apps to engage clients such as video uploads Tik-Tok style and video reveiws.

Facilities: We will provide prisoners with the necessary resources and equipment to create their art projects. This may include access to a workshop or studio, as well as materials such as paints, brushes, and canvases.

Staffing: We hope to hire volunteer mentors and support staff to work with our prisoners. This team will be responsible for providing guidance, support, and training to help prisoners develop their entrepreneurial skills and turn their art into a successful business.

Financial Projections:

Start-Up Costs: Our start-up costs will be minimal and include purchasing materials and equipment as well as marketing and advertising expenses. Many of the startup costs associated with a business can be avoided. This site was made by one person (me) who knows full-stack development. I'm also certified in the cloud and can manage servers and create API's. Furthermore, some equipment has been purchased already, including a machine that will help with mailing. As far as unexpected costs go, none should be inhibitory.

Revenue Projections: Our revenue projections are based on our sales projections and our commission model. We expect to generate waves of sales initially as we network, which will grow and stabalize over time as our program becomes more established and a breakout point on sales is reached.

Break-Even Point: Our break-even point will be reached when our revenues equal our expenses. We expect to reach this point within the first year of operations.

Conclusion:

Cell-Block Creative is a unique program that has the potential to make a significant impact on the lives of prisoners and their families, as well as on the larger community. By providing prisoners with the resources and support they need to turn their creativity into a successful business, we believe that our program has the potential to improve their financial situation, foster self-sufficiency, and reduce the risk of recidivism. With the right resources and support, we are confident that our program will be successful and have a lasting impact on the lives of those we serve.
